Pennantia Baylisiana
Three Kings Kaikōmako
Pennantia baylisiana, commonly known as Three Kings Kaikōmako and Kaikōmako Manawa Tāwhi is a species of plant in the family Pennantiaceae. It is endemic to the Three Kings Islands, New Zealand, where only one plant is known to exist. It is threatened by habitat loss.
Elaeocarpus Bojeri
Bois Dentelle
Elaeocarpus bojeri, locally known as a bois dentelle, descriptive of its delicate white flowers is a species of flowering plant in the Elaeocarpaceae family. The species was once only found close to an Indian temple at Grand Bassin in Mauritius, where fewer ten individuals were known to grow in the 1990s.
Tilia Americana
American Basswood
Tilia americana is a species of tree in the family Malvaceae, native to eastern North America, from southeast Manitoba east to New Brunswick, southwest to northeast Oklahoma, southeast to South Carolina, and west along the Niobrara River to Cherry County, Nebraska.
Carya Ovata
Shagbark Hickory
Carya ovata, the shagbark hickory, is a common hickory in the Eastern United States and southeast Canada. It is a large, deciduous tree, growing well over 100 ft tall, and can live more than 350 years. The tallest measured shagbark, located in Savage Gulf, Tennessee, is over 150 ft tall.
